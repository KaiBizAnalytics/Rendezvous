# Opportunity Solution Tree — v2

**Product:** AI Wedding Concierge (Rendezvous)
**Updated:** March 23, 2026
**Source:** AI persona interviews (Li Na, Arjun, Karen, Mateo, Jason M.) + real-person interviews (Chole, Jason, Nina)

---

## Desired Outcome

Reduce wedding planning time and stress by **50%** by helping couples navigate early-stage uncertainty, make confident decisions faster, visualize their wedding vision with realistic budget breakdowns, and organize planning in one place.

---

# Opportunities (User Problems From Interviews)

## 1. Early-Stage Uncertainty — The "0 → 1" Problem ★ NEW

Couples don't know where to start. They lack a mental model for what a wedding looks like end-to-end: what to book first, how long things take, what's realistic for their budget, and how all the pieces fit together.

**Evidence:**
- Chole: "The most stressful part is that I don't really know how all the pieces fit together."
- Nina: "At the beginning I had no direction — I didn't know what kind of wedding to have."
- Nina relied on a human consultant to provide "a big picture" and step-by-step guidance.
- Even couples who hire professional planners still feel lost in the early stages.

**Key insight:** The biggest pain point is not finding vendors — it's not knowing what you need, in what order, or at what cost. Couples need a structured starting point before they're ready for vendor discovery.

---

## 2. Decision Fatigue and Inspiration Overload

Couples must make hundreds of small decisions. Inspiration platforms (Instagram, Pinterest, Xiaohongshu) amplify this by presenting endless options with no way to filter by budget, feasibility, or personal context.

**Evidence:**
- Chole: "There is so much information online — everything looks good, so I want to include everything."
- Li Na: "The stress doesn't come from one big thing — it's the accumulation of hundreds of small decisions."
- Jason: "I don't know how to compare two photographers... they only show you their best work."
- Nina prefers "done-for-you" packages over making many individual decisions.

**Key insight:** Users want decision support and curated guidance, not more inspiration galleries or long checklists. One personalized vision is worth more than a thousand Pinterest pins.

---

## 3. Budget Allocation Is Opaque

Couples struggle to understand how to distribute their total budget across categories. Vendor pricing is hidden, and generic ranges from tools like ChatGPT are too broad to be useful. Couples cannot visualize trade-offs between spending priorities.

**Evidence:**
- Chole wants help distributing budget across food, decoration, photography, etc. based on her priorities.
- Nina: "ChatGPT gave me too wide of a budget range... it wasn't very useful."
- Nina specifically needs to visualize budget trade-offs.
- Jason: "It's very not transparent... you don't really know how to compare vendors."

**Key insight:** Budget guidance must be specific to the couple's profile (location, guest count, style), not generic. Couples need to see where their money goes visually — tied to tangible elements of their wedding, not abstract spreadsheet rows.

---

## 4. Fragmented Planning Tools

Planning happens across multiple disconnected tools — spreadsheets, Instagram, vendor websites, email threads, and planner-provided trackers. None of these tools connect ideas, timeline, budget, and logistics together.

**Evidence:**
- Chole: "My tracker is just a simple Excel sheet... I can list things, but I can't connect them together."
- Chole maintains multiple trackers simultaneously (personal spreadsheet + planner system).
- Li Na: Information scattered across Notion, Google Sheets, Instagram, vendor sites, and family messages.
- Jason: Planning data scattered across spreadsheets, media, and different platforms.

**Key insight:** Existing tools store information but don't help couples make decisions or see how choices affect each other. The opportunity is not "more features" but fewer, better-integrated ones.

---

## 5. Vendor Trust and Quality Assessment

Even after finding vendors, couples have no reliable way to evaluate quality or predict outcomes. Vendor portfolios are curated, reviews are unreliable, and there's no way to compare objectively.

**Evidence:**
- Jason: "I don't know how to compare two photographers... they only show you their best work."
- Jason: "I'd rather pay more just to feel confident and at ease."
- Jason: "If I still have to go outside your platform to find vendors, then I don't need your platform."
- Nina trusts her human consultant's vendor connections and insider knowledge.

**Key insight:** Vendor discovery alone isn't enough. Couples need decision confidence — quality signals, comparison frameworks, and transparent information that helps them commit without anxiety. Platform completeness is critical for trust.

---

## 6. AI Trust Gap

Couples are skeptical of AI recommendations that feel generic, overly broad, or disconnected from local context. Trust depends on specificity, visual proof, and the ability to replicate the structured guidance a human planner provides.

**Evidence:**
- Chole: "I still trust human judgment more than a system's taste."
- Nina: "I prefer a human wedding consultant because it's more worry-free."
- Nina: AI tools fail due to "overly broad outputs and requiring too many inputs."
- Jason wants platform completeness before trusting it.

**Key insight:** AI must earn trust through specificity, visual personalization, and showing — not just telling. A visual mockup of "your wedding" is more persuasive than a text list of recommendations.

---

## 7. Cultural & Personalization Gaps

Existing tools assume a Western wedding template. Multicultural couples face additional complexity in coordinating traditions, finding culturally aware vendors, and planning multi-ceremony events.

**Evidence:**
- Li Na: Balancing Western preferences with Chinese traditions like tea ceremonies and banquet dinners.
- Chole: Planning in Vancouver with cross-cultural considerations (Xiaohongshu as a planning source indicates Chinese cultural context).

**Key insight:** Cultural personalization should be woven into the core experience, not bolted on as a module. It informs venue selection, catering, ceremony structure, and budget allocation.

---

# Solutions (Product Ideas)

## 1. Interactive Wedding Vision Board with Budget Breakdown ★ HERO FEATURE — MVP

After completing the style questionnaire, couples see a personalized visual mockup (mood board) of their dream wedding ceremony with annotated budget callout boxes linked to specific elements in the scene. This is the moment the product earns trust and demonstrates value.

**How it works:**
1. User completes the questionnaire (venue type, guest count, budget, style preferences).
2. An **AI-generated ceremony scene image** is created in real time, reflecting the couple's specific selections — venue type, style, colour palette, and setting (e.g., an elegant outdoor garden ceremony with a floral arch and white chairs for a couple who selected outdoor + elegant + 50 guests).
3. A full-screen overlay appears displaying the generated image with annotated budget callout boxes, each connected to a visible element: Venue & Setup, Florals & Décor, Music & DJ, MC & Host, Photography & Video.
4. Each callout is clickable — expanding to show details, alternatives, or upgrade/downgrade options.
5. A total budget summary bar at the bottom shows allocated vs. remaining budget.

**Why this matters:**
- Solves the 0→1 problem by giving couples an immediate, tangible picture of **their** wedding — not a generic stock photo.
- AI-generated imagery creates a uniquely personal "wow" moment that no competitor offers.
- Makes budget allocation visual and intuitive instead of abstract spreadsheet rows.
- Earns AI trust by showing something genuinely personalized and specific, not generic text or templates.
- Reduces decision fatigue by presenting a curated starting point, not infinite options.
- Creates an emotional, shareable moment that drives word-of-mouth and engagement.

---

## 2. Personalized Vendor Matching — MVP

Instead of browsing large vendor directories, couples receive **5–10 curated recommendations** that match their profile. Emphasis shifts from quantity to decision confidence — each recommendation includes transparent pricing, quality signals, and comparison data.

Matching criteria: budget fit, guest capacity, wedding style, cultural requirements, location.

---

## 3. Visual Budget Allocation Engine — MVP

Budget guidance that goes beyond generic ranges. Uses the couple's location, guest count, style, and spending priorities to produce a specific, itemized budget breakdown. Visualized through the Vision Board and the planning dashboard.

Features: personalized budget splits by category, priority-weighted allocation, trade-off visualization, real wedding cost benchmarks for the couple's market.

---

## 4. Centralized Planning Hub — MVP

A single dashboard combining the Vision Board, vendor shortlist, budget tracker, and task checklist. Replaces the fragmented multi-tool workflow.

**Key design principle:** Tools must be connected — changing a budget allocation should update vendor recommendations; shortlisting a vendor should update the budget view. Information should never be siloed.

---

## 5. Cultural Wedding Planning Integration — MVP

Cultural awareness woven into the core experience rather than offered as optional modules. Questionnaire captures cultural context upfront; recommendations, budget templates, and vendor matching adapt accordingly.

Examples: Chinese tea ceremony and banquet structures, Indian multi-day event planning, multicultural ceremony coordination, LGBTQ+-friendly vendor filtering.

---

## 6. AI Wedding Concierge Chat — FUTURE (Post-MVP)

A conversational AI chatbot that guides couples through planning with structured, step-by-step support — replicating the experience of working with a human wedding consultant.

Capabilities:
- Early-stage guidance ("What should I book first?", "Is $30k realistic for 70 guests in Vancouver?")
- Budget-aware recommendations grounded in the couple's profile
- Cultural customization for ceremony and vendor needs
- Vendor discovery filtered by budget, capacity, location, and style
- Decision support: pros/cons, trade-off analysis, "If you spend more here, you'll need to reduce there"

**Why deferred:** The Vision Board with budget breakdown, personalized vendor matching, and centralized planning hub already address the core user problems identified in research. A conversational chatbot introduces additional complexity (LLM integration, prompt engineering, streaming UX, trust calibration) that can be layered in after the foundation is validated. The MVP earns trust through visual, structured experiences first — a conversational layer can amplify that trust once couples are already engaged.

# Assumption Tests (Experiments)

## Test 1: Vision Board Engagement ★ MVP

Show the interactive Vision Board with budget breakdown after questionnaire completion. Measure whether couples spend time exploring the callout boxes and whether it increases onboarding completion rate vs. going directly to a vendor list.

**Success signal:** >60% of users who complete the questionnaire interact with at least one budget callout box.

## Test 2: Budget Specificity Trust ★ MVP

Compare user trust and satisfaction between generic budget ranges ("$20k–$50k") and profile-specific budget breakdowns ("Based on your 70 guests, outdoor venue in Vancouver, elegant style: Venue $15k, Florals $4k..."). Measure whether specific numbers increase platform engagement.

**Success signal:** Users receiving specific breakdowns rate trust ≥4/5 vs. ≤3/5 for generic ranges.

## Test 3: Recommendation Value — MVP

Show users 5 personalized vendor recommendations and measure if they prefer this over browsing vendor directories.

**Success signal:** >70% of users prefer curated recommendations.

## Test 4: Cultural Personalization — MVP

Prototype a culturally-aware onboarding flow and test whether recommendations feel relevant to multicultural couples.

**Success signal:** Multicultural users rate recommendation relevance ≥4/5.

## Test 5: Concierge Interaction — FUTURE (Post-MVP)

Test whether couples engage more with structured guidance prompts ("Here's what to do this month") vs. open-ended chat ("Ask me anything").

**Success signal:** Guided prompts drive >2x interaction rate vs. open chat.
